Aisha Khan

Navigating Diagnosis & Emotional Health

“For years I dismissed my irregular periods and sudden weight gain as stress from college. When I was finally diagnosed with PCOS, I felt lost — I didn’t even know what questions to ask. Through this community, I found stories that mirrored my own and resources that explained PCOS in plain language. Slowly, I began journaling my symptoms, joining the monthly webinars, and connecting with others on the same path. Today, I’m not only managing my symptoms better, but I’ve also regained the confidence to talk about reproductive health without shame.”

Priya Menon

Overcoming Workplace Stigma

“When I started working at a corporate firm in Bengaluru, fatigue and mood swings made it difficult to keep up. I often faced judgment for taking sick days or avoiding office parties due to my diet restrictions. Finding this website was like breathing again — I read expert articles on managing PCOS at work, and the forum gave me scripts to explain my condition confidently to HR. A few months later, my company even introduced a women’s wellness awareness session inspired by our discussions. The change started small, but it mattered.”

Neha Patel

Lifestyle Success & Management

“After my diagnosis, I tried every diet I saw online, but nothing stuck. Joining the PCOS Awareness Hub taught me that balance is more important than perfection. I used their repository to find research-backed nutrition advice and began following realistic exercise routines shared by other Indian women. Over six months, I built a routine that fits my lifestyle. The biggest win? My energy levels improved, and my periods became more regular — but most importantly, I stopped feeling alone in my journey.”

About Our Mission

We are a non-profit community focused solely on bridging the information gap for individuals affected by PCOS. Our mission is to provide easily digestible, evidence-based health resources and foster a supportive environment where personal stories drive collective action against diagnostic delays and social stigma.
